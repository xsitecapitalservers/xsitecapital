Unlocking Your Wealth: The Ultimate Guide to Tax Strategy and Planning

If you feel like you are paying too much in taxes, you probably are. Here is everything shared in one of our most-requested education sessions — so you can keep more of what you earn and invest it back into your future.

XSITE Capital Education Series — Tax Strategy & Planning with Laxman & Cody from DocWealth

In a recent education series, XSITE Capital sat down with Laxman and Cody from DocWealth to break down the crucial differences between simply filing your taxes and proactively planning them. 01:25

Here is a breakdown of the knowledge, tips, Q&A, and value pieces shared during this insightful session — to help you keep more of what you earn.

1

The Foundation of Tax Strategy

Filing vs. Planning
Tax filing is reactive — it looks at the past year. Tax planning is proactive — it looks ahead to reduce your future effective tax rate. Proper tax planning yields a significantly higher ROI than handing documents to your CPA in April. 02:45
Entity Structuring
The way you structure your income matters. For 1099 contractors or business owners, creating an LLC, PLLC, or S-Corp can offer substantial tax advantages to lower your taxable income. 04:10
The S-Corp Nuance
An S-Corp can save money on self-employment taxes, but you must pay yourself a "reasonable salary." Many professionals forget to actually file the S-Corp election form, leaving significant money on the table. 09:41
Kids on Payroll
Hiring your children can be a fantastic tax deduction — but it must be legitimate. They must be of working age, perform real business tasks, be paid a reasonable wage, and go through official payroll. 08:14
Real Estate & Depreciation
Real estate investing offers massive tax benefits, particularly through cost segregation and accelerated depreciation. Oil and gas investments can offer up to a 100% deduction in year one. 04:53
The Augusta Rule
This rule allows you to rent your primary residence for up to 14 days a year without recognizing the rental income on your tax return. One of the most underused provisions in the tax code. 39:57
Retirement Vehicles
Beyond the standard 401(k), high earners with 1099 income should explore Solo 401(k)s, SEP IRAs, and Cash Balance Plans to maximize tax-deferred growth. 53:10
2

Live Q&A: Your Top Tax Questions Answered

Can cost segregation losses offset IRA or Roth distributions? 18:20

It depends on your status. Passive losses from real estate generally only offset passive income. To offset active income — like distributions or W2 earnings — you typically need to qualify as a Real Estate Professional.

Does Real Estate Professional Status (REPS) make losses active? 23:47

Yes. If you meet the requirement of 750 hours and material participation, your passive real estate losses become active — allowing them to offset your W2 income and other earnings.

What is best practice for amending past taxes? 20:05

You generally have three years to amend a past return. It is worth doing if you missed a major deduction — like failing to calculate depreciation on an asset — that materially changes your tax outcome.

Can you have both a SEP IRA and a Solo 401(k)? 32:32

Generally, no — you cannot have both for the same business. There are nuanced exceptions if you have separate streams of income, but this requires careful structuring with your CPA.

Are aircraft purchases tax-deductible? 33:09

Yes — but these are among the most highly scrutinized deductions by the IRS. You must be able to strictly prove the business use of the aircraft with meticulous documentation.

Is it better to lease or buy a vehicle for a business? 44:23

It comes down to math and timing. Buying often allows accelerated depreciation — a large deduction upfront. Leasing spreads the deduction over the term. Do what makes financial sense first, then optimize the tax benefit second.

3

Top Tips for Immediate Implementation

01
Communicate Year-Round 09:00
Do not wait until April to talk to your CPA. Communicate at least quarterly to make proactive moves before the year ends. Reactive filing leaves money behind.
02
Multi-State Awareness 11:33
If you are a 1099 worker traveling across state lines, do not ignore multi-state tax laws. You are likely required to file and pay taxes in every state where you perform work.
03
Document Everything 50:43
Do not fear an audit if your deductions are legitimate. Keep meticulous logs of miles, hours, and expenses. You can never over-document.
04
Trapped in a W2? Find a Side Hustle 28:18
W2 employees are highly limited in deductions. Starting a 1099 side gig or investing in real estate opens up a whole new world of tax shelters and retirement accounts.
4

The Big Picture

The overarching theme was clear: it is not just about making money — it is about how much you keep.

As a W2 employee, you have very little control over your tax burden. As a business owner or 1099 earner, the tax code rewards you for investing back into the economy.

Structuring your business correctly from day one keeps you out of audit trouble and is an investment that yields immense ROI over the course of your career. 06:28

The goal of tax strategy is to create a surplus of capital. Once you save those dollars from going to the IRS, the final piece of the puzzle is deploying them into wealth-building vehicles — like institutional-quality real estate — to compound your wealth faster. 1:01:12

Ready to stop overpaying the IRS? Start treating your tax strategy as a vital pillar of your wealth-building journey today.
